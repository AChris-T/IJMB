About IJMB

Interim Joint Matriculation Board(IJMB)

The Interim Joint Matriculation Board Examination (IJMBE) serves as a qualifying test for admission into both Nigerian and international universities. Specifically, the IJMB examination focuses on Advanced Level subjects, enabling students to gain Direct Entry into the second year (200 level) of university studies.

The syllabus for the IJMB Examination aligns with global educational standards and the admission criteria of various universities, both within Nigeria and abroad.

The IJMB syllabus is intended to be completed within a single academic year, spanning two semesters. It is organized to ensure that all topics within the IJMB subjects are thoroughly covered during these two semesters. At the end of each subject syllabus, a list of recommended reading materials is provided to assist institutions, lecturers, and students throughout the IJMB program.

To effectively cover the IJMB syllabus, it is recommended to have a minimum of 8-10 lecture hours per week, which includes practical sessions and fieldwork. The IJMB syllabus is subject to review and may be updated every eight years.

Why Choose Us
01

Approval

IJMB is a government-approved national educational program managed by Ahmadu Bello University, Zaria, and supported by various affiliated Study Centres across Nigeria. The program offers a pathway for successful candidates to gain direct admission into the second year (200 Level) at universities both in Nigeria and abroad.

02

Trend

Each year, approximately 30,000 candidates enroll in the IJMB program. Of these, around 20,000 successfully complete the program and use their IJMB results to gain admission into the second year (200 Level) at various universities across the country.

03

Certification

The IJMBE is officially recognized by the Nigeria University Commission (NUC) and the Joint Admission Matriculation Board (JAMB) as an Advanced Level program. Upon successful completion, candidates are eligible for direct admission into the second year (200 Level) at universities.

Additional Information

Subject Combination

IJMB Advanced Level Examination candidates should consult the current JAMB brochure to determine the suitable IJMB subject combinations for their intended university courses. Each IJMB candidate is required to enroll in and take only three (3) subjects for the final examination.

Requirements & Cost

Applicants need credit passes in five subjects, including major subjects, in no more than two sittings of SSCE/GCE exams by WAEC, NECO, or NABTEB. Those awaiting results can apply online by selecting "Awaiting Result."
The IJMB program lasts nine months, divided into two semesters, and is open to all candidates nationwide. Applicants should choose their preferred study centre during online registration, with early registration advised due to limited availability.
